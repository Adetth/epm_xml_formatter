2. Foundation: Hierarchy & Dimensions

The bedrock of EPBCS is dimensional design. Getting Dense/Sparse configurations wrong results in massive block sizes, extreme memory overhead, and unworkable performance.

  • •
    Dense vs. Sparse: Typically, Account and Period must be Dense. Entity, Scenario, Version, and Custom dimensions (Product, Project) must be Sparse. This minimizes block size and maximizes block density.
  • •
    The Hourglass Design: Hierarchies should resemble an hourglass. Few nodes at the top (Total Company), expanding slightly in the middle (Divisions), and expanding vastly at the leaf level (Cost Centers). Avoid flat, wide structures under a single parent.
  • •
    Dynamic Calc Strategy: Upper-level Sparse members should generally be Dynamic Calc (if logic is simple aggregation). Upper-level Dense members are often calculated via scripts to save memory, though simple rollups can be Dynamic.

Naming Conventions & Member Efficiency

A rigid naming convention is critical for script writability and data integration. Never use raw names as member names; use aliases for user readability.

Dimension Type Member Name (System) Alias (User Facing)
Entity E_100 North America Region
Account A_40100 Software Revenue
Custom (Project) P_ERP_Impl ERP Implementation

Note: Avoid spaces, special characters, and excessive length in member names to optimize internal ESSBASE indexing.

3. Domain Architectures: Context is King

A standard framework fails when applied blindly across industries. To build a senior-approved application, you must speak the client's language and configure modules based on their distinct financial drivers. The chart below visualizes the shifting focus areas required for different client domains.

Conventional Banking (e.g., ENBD)

Focus: Net Interest Margin (NIM), Yield Curves, Funds Transfer Pricing (FTP).
Architecture: Requires complex custom dimensions for Product (Loans vs. Deposits) and Currency. Planning is highly driver-based, calculating interest income based on projected rates spread over term structures. Non-Performing Loan (NPL) provisions are critical.

Islamic Banking (e.g., Mashreq Al Islami)

Focus: Sharia Compliance, Profit Distribution, Asset-Backed Structures.
Architecture: Fundamentally different from conventional banking. Zero interest metrics. Planning is based on Profit Rates and contracts (Murabaha, Mudaraba, Ijarah). Requires specific EPBCS modeling for 'Pool Management' where depositor funds are linked to specific earning assets to calculate equitable profit distribution. Zakat calculation modules are mandatory.

Mining & Extraction (e.g., Ivanhoe Mines)

Focus: Life of Mine (LOM), Capital Expenditure (CAPEX), Commodity Volatility.
Architecture: Heavy reliance on the EPBCS Capital Module. Massive investments in shaft sinking and processing plants mapped out over 20+ years. Driver-based planning focuses on 'Cost per Ton' extracted, ore grade degradation over time, and macroeconomic scenario planning for volatile metal pricing (e.g., Copper, Gold) and FX exposure.

4. Efficiency & Performance Benchmarks

High-level application delivery is judged by speed. An elegant model that takes hours to consolidate is a failure. Utilizing targeted data movement and modern scripting techniques is non-negotiable.

Groovy
Targeted Calculations

Execute rules ONLY on edited cells rather than entire database blocks. Reduces save times by up to 90%.

FIX Statements
Block Control

Always FIX on Sparse dimensions. Never FIX on Dense dimensions; let the calculation engine sweep the dense blocks.

Clear Data
Before Loading

Always run a clear script on target regions before executing data loads or major aggregations to prevent block fragmentation.
